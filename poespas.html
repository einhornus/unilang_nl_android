poespas (44838)


Etymology

Ablauting reduplication of poes, which is thought to have had an original meaning of “muck”. Compare German Puspas, English pishpash, mishmash.

Noun

poespas m (plural poespassen, diminutive poespasje n)

  1. fuss, ado, hubbub
  2. (obsolete) mash, stew [17th - 19th c.]

Usage notes

  • Often used in fixed phrases:
zonder (te veel) poespas — without (too much) ado
alle poespas over ... — all the fuss about ...

Synonyms

  • heisa
  • kouwe drukte
  • roerom
  • rommelzooi
  • omhaal
