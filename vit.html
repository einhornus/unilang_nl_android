vit (55533)


Etymology 1

From Proto-Germanic *witją from Proto-Indo-European *weyd- (“see, know”). Cognate to English wit, archaic Dutch wit, akin to Old Saxon giwit.

Noun

vit n (genitive singular vits, uncountable)

  1. intelligence
  2. consciousness
    • fáa vitið aftur
      to regain consciousness
Synonyms
  • (intelligence): skilningur, fatan, skyn, skil

Etymology 2

From Old Norse vit.

Pronoun

vit

  1. we
    • Vit eru føroyingar.
      We are Faroese.
    • Vit koma aftur.
      We come back. / We will return.
Synonyms
  • okur (Sandoy, Suðuroy)

Werkwoord

vit

  1. enkelvoud tegenwoordige tijd van vitten
  2. gebiedende wijs van vitten
