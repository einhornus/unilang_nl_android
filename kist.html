kist (2180)


Etymology 1

From Middle Dutch kiste, from Proto-Germanic *kistō, from Latin cista, from Ancient Greek κίστη (kístē), from Proto-Indo-European *kisteh₂.

Noun

kist f or m (plural kisten, diminutive kistje n)

  1. box, chest
  2. (informal) a boot or large shoe, especially an army boot
  3. coffin
    Synonyms: doodskist, lijkkist
  4. (aviation) aeroplane
