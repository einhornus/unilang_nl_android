kans (385)


Etymology

From Middle Dutch canse, borrowed from Old Northern French cance, from Vulgar Latin *cadentia, from cadō (“to fall”), referring to the falling of dice.

Cognate with French chance, English chance.

Noun

kans f (plural kansen, diminutive kansje n)

  1. chance
  2. opportunity
  3. (mathematics) probability
