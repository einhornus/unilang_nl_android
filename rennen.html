rennen (1018)


Etymology 1

A merger of two related verbs:

  • Middle Dutch rinnen (“to flow, to run fast”), from Old Dutch *rinnan, from Proto-Germanic *rinnaną.
  • Middle Dutch rennen (“to run/ride fast”), from Old Dutch *rennen, from Proto-Germanic *rannijaną.

Verb

rennen

  1. (intransitive, about people or animals) to run, to move fast
  2. (intransitive, about fluids) to run, flow, stream
  3. (intransitive, about fluids) to flow together, combine, thicken
  4. (intransitive) to leak, seep through
Synonyms
  • (to move fast): hardlopen, lopen
Derived terms
  • renner
  • wedrennen
  • hondenrennen
  • paardenrennen

Etymology 2

Noun

rennen

  1. Plural form of ren

Werkwoord

rennen

  1. inergatief (ongericht) zeer snel lopen
    • Je hoeft niet te rennen, we hebben alle tijd.
  2. ergatief (gericht) zeer snel lopen
    • Ik ben naar huis gerend.
