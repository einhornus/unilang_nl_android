met (25)


Etymology 1

From Old Dutch mit, variant of *midi (from which mee, mede), from Proto-Germanic *midi.

Preposition

met

  1. with, along with (another person)
  2. with, using (a tool, instrument or other means)
  3. at, for, during (a holiday/festivity)
    Heb je zin om met kerst bij ons langs te komen?
    Do you fancy visiting us for Christmas?
Inflection
Pronominal adverbs of met

Etymology 2

From Middle Dutch met, from Proto-Germanic *matjÄ…, whence also German Mett (through Low German). Related with Proto-Germanic *matiz, whence English meat.

Noun

met n or m (uncountable)

  1. (dated, Eastern Netherlands) mince (sometimes specifically uncooked)
Verwante begrippen
  • mee, mede
Afgeleide begrippen
  • metgezel, metterdaad, metterdag, metterhaast, mettertijd, metterwoon, tranen-met-tuitentelevisie

Zelfstandig naamwoord

met o

  1. arch. stukjes die overblijven bij het snijden van grotere stukken vlees.
Afgeleide begrippen
  • metworst, ondermet
