faun (47447)


Etymology

From Latin Faunus.

Noun

faun (plural fauns)

  1. (Roman mythology) A woodland creature with pointed ears, legs, and short horns of a goat and a fondness for unrestrained revelry.
    Synonym: satyr
  2. (entomology) Any of various nymphalid butterflies of the genus Faunis.
