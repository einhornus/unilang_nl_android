roet (18356)


Etymology

From Middle Dutch roet, from Old Dutch *rōt, from Proto-Germanic *hrōtaz (“soot”).

Noun

roet n (uncountable)

  1. soot (fine dark particles of amorphous carbon and tar, produced by the incomplete combustion of organic material or fuel)
  2. a fungus type

Derived terms

  • roetachtig
  • roetbruin n
  • roetdeeltje n
  • roeten (verb)
  • roetkleur
  • roetkool
  • roetmop
