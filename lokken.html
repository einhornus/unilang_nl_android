lokken (4229)


Etymology 1

From Middle Dutch locken, from Old Dutch loccon, from Proto-Germanic *lukkōną.

Verb

lokken

  1. (transitive) to attract, lure, bait
    in de val lokken
    to lure into the trap
Inflection

Etymology 2

See the etymology of the main entry.

Noun

lokken
