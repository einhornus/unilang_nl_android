zinnen (5291)


Etymology 1

From Middle Dutch sinnen, from Old Dutch *sinnan, from Proto-West Germanic *sinnan, from Proto-Indo-European *sent-n-, a nasal suffix present from the root *sent- (“to feel”).

Verb

zinnen

  1. (intransitive) to contemplate, to plot
Derived terms
  • bezinnen
  • verzinnen

Etymology 2

From zin (“desire”) + | -en.

Verb

zinnen

  1. (transitive) to satisfy, to please
    Dat zint mij niet.
    I am not satisfied with that.

Etymology 3

See the etymology of the main entry.

Noun

zinnen

  1. Plural form of zin

Werkwoord

zinnen

  1. inergatief de gedachten ergens over laten gaan
    • Hij zon op wraak.
  2. onpersoonlijk in de smaak vallen
    • Dat zinde hem helemaal niet.
