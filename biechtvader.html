biechtvader (28870)


Etymology

From Middle Dutch biechtvāder. Equivalent to biecht (“confession”) + | vader (“father, priest”).

Noun

biechtvader m (plural biechtvaders, diminutive biechtvadertje n)

  1. A confessor, priest who administers the sacrament of confession, ad hoc or on a more regular basis with specific believers (e.g. his parishioners)

Zelfstandig naamwoord

biechtvader m

  1. een priester die biechten afneemt
