trommel (15602)


Etymology

From Middle Dutch trommel, ultimately of imitative origin.

Noun

trommel f or m (plural trommels, diminutive trommeltje n)

  1. (music) drum, a type of percussion instrument
  2. (technology) drum, various, often cylindrically shaped objects or components
  3. cylinder (of a revolver)
  4. a box to hold cookies in

Synonyms

  • (music instrument): drum, trom, bong

Derived terms

  • broodjetrommel
  • koektrommel

  • trommelrem
  • trommelstok
  • trommelvlies

Verb

trommel

  1. first-person singular present indicative of trommelen
  2. imperative of trommelen

Zelfstandig naamwoord

trommel v/m

  2. een vaak ronde, afsluitbare, gewoonlijk metalen doos
