verschalken (52863)


Etymology

From Middle Dutch verschalken (“to subjugate, to mislead, to outwit”). Equivalent to ver- + | schalk (“scoundrel”) + | -en.

Verb

verschalken

  1. (transitive) to eat up, to catch and eat
    Uiteindelijk wist de tijgerin haar lichtovetige prooi te verschalken.
    In the end the tigress managed to capture and devour her light-footed prey.
  2. (transitive) to mislead, to fool
  3. (transitive) to catch by means of cunning, to trap, to capture, to ensnare
  4. (transitive) to ambush

Synonyms

  • (mislead): misleiden, bedotten, belazeren
  • (eat): verorberen
