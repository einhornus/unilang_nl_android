poel (16051)


Etymology

From Middle Dutch poel, from Old Dutch *puol, from Proto-Germanic *p≈çlaz.

Noun

poel m (plural poelen, diminutive poeltje n)

  1. a standing body of water; pool, puddle
  2. (obsolete, literary) abyss, chasm
  3. (obsolete) hell
