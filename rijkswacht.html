rijkswacht (44755)


Etymology

A Belgian Dutch neologism from 1932, derived from rijk (“realm”) + | wacht (“guard”) for French gendarmerie.

Noun

rijkswacht f (plural rijkswachten, diminutive rijkswachtje n)

  1. A (former) Belgian military body charged with police duties among the civilian population, the Belgian Dutch / Flemish translation of the gendarmerie.

Related terms

(Netherlands) marechaussee.
