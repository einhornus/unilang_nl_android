gaar (11959)


Etymology

From Middle Dutch gaer (“ready”), from Old Dutch garo (“ready”), from Proto-Germanic *garwaz (“ready”). More at yare.

Adjective

gaar (comparative gaarder, superlative gaarst)

  1. (of food, esp. meat and vegetables) well-cooked; done; ready for consumption
  2. (figuratively, of a person) finished, exhausted

Derived terms

  • gaarkeuken

Verb

gaar

  1. first-person singular present indicative of garen
  2. imperative of garen
