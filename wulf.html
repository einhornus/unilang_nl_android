wulf (58493)


Etymology

From Proto-Germanic *wulfaz, from Proto-Indo-European *wĺ̥kʷos. Germanic cognates include Old Frisian wolf, Old Saxon wulf, Old Dutch wulf, Old High German wolf, Old Norse úlfr, Gothic 𐍅𐌿𐌻𐍆𐍃 (wulfs). The Indo-European root is also the source of Avestan 𐬬𐬆𐬵𐬭𐬐𐬀 | (vəhrka), Lithuanian vilkas, Old Church Slavonic влькъ (vlĭkŭ). Ancient Greek λύκος (lúkos) and Latin lupus also probably from the same root, either internally borrowed or with metathesis because of a wolf taboo.
