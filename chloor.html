chloor (23891)


Etymology

Borrowed from French chlore, formed from English chlorine, which was coined by Humphry Davy based on Ancient Greek χλωρός (khlōrós).

Noun

chloor n (uncountable)

  1. chlorine [from early 19th c.]
    Synonym: chlorine

Synonyms

  • zeezoutstof
  • zoutstof
