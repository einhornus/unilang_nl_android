mokken (13556)


Etymology 1

Related to Old High German muckezzen (“to pout”), Middle Low German mucken (“to grouse, to grumble”), ultimately of West Germanic onomatopoeic origin. Probably not related to Latin mugio (“I bellow, moo, bray”).

Verb

mokken

  1. to sulk
    Synonym: pruilen
Inflection

Etymology 2

Onomatopoeic.

Verb

mokken

  1. to dook (ferret sound)
Inflection

See the etymology of the main entry.

mokken
