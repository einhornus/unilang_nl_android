tinnitus (87331)


Etymology

From Latin tinnītus (“a jingling, ringing”).

Noun

tinnitus (countable and uncountable, plural tinnituses)

  1. (pathology) The perception of noise, such as a ringing or beating sound, which has no external source.

Synonyms

  • acouasm
