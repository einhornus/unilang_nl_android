stig (29616)


Etymology 1

From Proto-Germanic *stīgō, from *stīganą (“climb”) ( gt; Old English stīgan). Cognate with Middle Dutch stige, Old High German stiga. A masculine Germanic variant *stīgaz is indicated by Old High German stic (German Steig), Old Norse stígr (Swedish stig).

Noun

stīġ f (nominative plural stīġa or stīġe)

  1. path (especially steep or narrow)

Etymology 2

From Proto-Germanic *stiją. Cognate with Old Norse stí (Danish sti).

Noun

stiġ n (nominative plural stiġ)

  1. hall, building, sty
