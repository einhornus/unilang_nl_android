scherp (2715)


Etymology 1

From Middle Dutch scharp, scherp, from Old Dutch skarp, from Proto-Germanic *skarpaz.

Adjective

scherp (comparative scherper, superlative scherpst)

  1. sharp, able to cut easily
  2. alert, on the ball
  3. clever, intelligent, witty
  4. acute, having a small angle
  5. in focus, clearly visible, not blurry
  6. (of food) hot, spicy
Inflection

Etymology 2

See the etymology of the main entry.

Verb

scherp

  1. first-person singular present indicative of scherpen
  2. imperative of scherpen
