verstenen (55574)


Etymology 1

Some versteend (petrified) material

From Middle Dutch verstênen. Equivalent to ver- + | steen + | -en.

Verb

verstenen

  1. (transitive) to petrify, turn (something) into rock
  2. (by extension) to harden, make insensitive
  3. (intransitive) to be petrified, turned into rock
  4. (by extension) to be hardened, to become rigid, implacable
  5. (transitive) to equip, fit (a mill) with a(nother) millstone
Inflection

Etymology 2

From ver- + | stenen (“to moan, complain”).

Verb

verstenen

  1. (intransitive) To moan, complain
  2. (transitive) To bemoan, regret
Inflection

Werkwoord

verstenen

  1. ergatief zo hard worden als steen
    • Barnsteen is boomhars die in verloop van vele jaren versteend is.
  2. ergatief overdrachtelijk stijf worden van de kou of de schrik
    • Bij de aanblik daarvan versteende zij volledig.
