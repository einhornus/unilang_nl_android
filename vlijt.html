vlijt (62396)


Etymology

From Middle Dutch vlijt, from Old Dutch *flīt, , from Proto-West Germanic *flīt, from Proto-Germanic *flītaz, *flītiz (“diligence, effort”), related to Proto-Germanic *flītaną (“to strive”) (Cognate with English flite, German Low German Fliet, Saterland Frisian Fliet, Dutch vlijt, Danish flid, Norwegian Bokmål flid, Norwegian Nynorsk flit, Swedish flit, and German Fleiß, Fleiss).

Noun

vlijt f or m (uncountable)

  1. diligence

Derived terms

  • vlijtig
