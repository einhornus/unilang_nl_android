hippocampus (51704)


Etymology

From Late Latin hippocampus, from Ancient Greek ἱππόκαμπος (hippókampos), from ῐ̔́ππος (híppos, “horse”) + | κάμπος (kámpos, “sea-monster”). The anatomy sense is so named from its resemblance to the seahorse.

Noun

hippocampus (plural hippocampi)

  1. (mythology) A mythological creature with the front head and forelimbs of a horse and the rear of a dolphin.
    Synonym: hippocamp
  2. (anatomy) A part of the brain located inside the temporal lobe, consisting mainly of grey matter. It is a component of the limbic system and plays a role in memory and emotion.

Synonyms

  • (neuroanatomy): HIPP

Derived terms

  • hippocampal
  • hippocampic
