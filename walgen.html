walgen (23979)


Etymology

From Middle Dutch walgen, from Old Dutch *walgon, from Proto-Germanic *walgōną (“to roll”).

Verb

walgen

  1. (archaic) to cause to become nauseated, to disgust.
    Het walgt mij. - It makes me sick.
  2. (used with van) to abhor, to hate
    Ik walg van jou. - I abhor you.

Inflection

Werkwoord

walgen

  1. inergatief hevige afkeer voelen voor iets
