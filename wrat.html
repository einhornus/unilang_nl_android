wrat (25502)


Etymology

From Middle Dutch wratte, from Old Dutch wratt, *wratta, *warta, from Proto-Germanic *wart«≠. The phonetic development is irregular, but compare Middle Low German wratte, Middle English wrette. Some modern Dutch dialects have the expected form wa(a)rt. Compare also German Warze, English wart.

Noun

wrat f (plural wratten, diminutive wratje n)

  1. wart (type of growth occurring on the skin)

Zelfstandig naamwoord

wrat v/m

  1. (medisch) hard, ruw knobbeltje op de huid veroorzaakt door virussen, vaak voorkomend op handen en voeten
