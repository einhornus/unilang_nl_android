kluiven (74929)


Etymology 1

From Middle Dutch cluven, from Old Dutch *klūvon, from Proto-Germanic *klūbōną. Originally a weak verb, it became strong by analogy with other verbs.

Verb

kluiven

  1. to gnaw, to nibble
  2. to bite (a piece of meat) into smaller chunks
Derived terms
  • afkluiven
