vaak (512)


Etymology 1

From Middle Dutch vake (“often”), from Old Dutch faka, dative singular of *fak (“compartment”) (modern vak).

Adverb

vaak (comparative vaker, superlative vaakst)

  1. often
Synonyms
  • dikwijls
  • veel

Etymology 2

From the adverb.

Adjective

vaak (not comparable)

  1. frequent, regular

Etymology 3

From Middle Dutch vake, from Old Dutch facon. Compare English fag.

Noun

vaak f (uncountable)

  1. sleepiness
Synonyms
  • slaap
Derived terms
  • Klaas Vaak
