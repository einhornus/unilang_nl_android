raket (3531)


Etymology 1

Borrowed from German Rakete, from Italian rocchetta, from rochetto (“rocket”, literally “a bobbin”), diminutive of rocca (“a distaff”), of Germanic origin, perhaps Gothic rukka, from Proto-Germanic *rukkô (“a distaff, a staff with flax fibres tied loosely to it, used in spinning thread”). Cognate with Old High German rocco, rocko, roccho, rocho ("a distaff"; gt; German Rocken (“a distaff”)), Swedish rock (“a distaff”), Icelandic rokkur (“a distaff”), Middle English rocke (“a distaff”). More at rock⁴.

Noun

raket f or m (plural raketten, diminutive raketje n)

  1. rocket, missile
  2. firework
  3. a type of ice cream in the shape of a rocket

Etymology 2

Borrowed from Middle French roquette, from Italian ruchetta, diminutive of ruca, from Latin eruca. Cognate to arugula.

Noun

raket f (plural raketten, diminutive raketje n)

  1. (botany) a rocket (type of plant)

Etymology 3

Borrowed from English racket, from French raquette. Previously borrowed from Middle French raquette.

Noun
