pulsen (44806)


Etymology

It is derived from the name of a moving company Abraham Puls amp; Sons, employed by the Nazi-led Dutch police to pillage and empty the homes of the 140,000 Jews of the Netherlands during the Holocaust.

Verb

pulsen

  1. (slang) to violently dispossess, to loot, to destroy property and environment, of Jews in particular.

Inflection
