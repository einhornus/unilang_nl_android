lijfarts (49068)


Etymology

From lijf (“body”) + | arts (“physician”), calque of German Leibarzt. First attested in the 17th century.

Noun

lijfarts m (plural lijfartsen, diminutive lijfartsje n)

  1. A personal physician (as attached to a prince, statesman etc.).
