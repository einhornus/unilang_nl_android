poep (8028)


Etymology 1

Noun

poep f (plural poepen, diminutive poepje n)

  1. (uncountable) shit
    Synonyms: drek, schijt, kak
  2. fart
    Synonym: scheet

Etymology 2

From French poupe (“poop, stern”). Ultimately from Latin puppis.

Noun

poep f (plural poepen, diminutive poepje n)

  1. (nautical) a vessel's stern
  2. (colloquial, Belgium) butt, bottom, behind, rear-end, hiney, tush
    Synonyms: achterste, achtereind, achterwerk, gat, kont, reet

Etymology 3

See the etymology of the main entry.

Verb

poep

  1. first-person singular present indicative of poepen
  2. imperative of poepen
