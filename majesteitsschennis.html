majesteitsschennis (103328)


Etymology

From majesteit + | -s- + | schennis.

Noun

majesteitsschennis f (plural majesteitsschennissen, diminutive majesteitsschennisje n)

  1. A lese majesty, high crime against the person of a monarch, or the monarchy or crown; by extension even against any sovereign state (which in Roman law was the original holder of 'majesty', only later a ruler's title)

Related terms

  • majesteitelijk
  • knapenschennis
