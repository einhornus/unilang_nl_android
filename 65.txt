<p>From <span class="etylcleanup"><span class="etyl"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Medieval_Latin" class="extiw" title="w:Medieval Latin">Medieval Latin</a></span></span> <i class="Latn mention" lang="la">crānium</i> <span class="mention-gloss-paren annotation-paren">(</span><span class="mention-gloss-double-quote">“</span><span class="mention-gloss">skull</span><span class="mention-gloss-double-quote">”</span><span class="mention-gloss-paren annotation-paren">)</span>, from <span class="etylcleanup"><span class="etyl"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ancient_Greek" class="extiw" title="w:Ancient Greek">Ancient Greek</a></span></span> <i class="polytonic mention" lang="grc">κρανίον</i> <span class="mention-gloss-paren annotation-paren">(</span><span lang="grc-Latn" class="mention-tr tr Latn">kraníon</span>, <span class="mention-gloss-double-quote">“</span><span class="mention-gloss">skull</span><span class="mention-gloss-double-quote">”</span><span class="mention-gloss-paren annotation-paren">)</span>.
</p>
<hr><h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Noun">Noun</span><span class="mw-editsection"></span></h3>
<p><strong class="Latn headword" lang="en"><a href = vnd.unilang:cranium.html>cranium</a></strong> (<i>plural</i> <b class="Latn form-of lang-en p-form-of" lang="en">craniums</b> <i>or</i> <b class="Latn form-of lang-en p-form-of" lang="en">crania</b>)
</p>
<ol><li><span class="ib-brac">(</span><span class="ib-content">anatomy</span><span class="ib-brac">)</span> The braincase or neurocranium; that part of the skull consisting of the <a href = vnd.unilang:bones.html>bones</a> enclosing the brain, but not including the <a href = vnd.unilang:bones.html>bones</a> of the face or jaw.
<ul><li><b>1858</b>, <a href = vnd.unilang:henry.html>Henry</a> Gray, <i>Anatomy: Descriptive and Surgical</i>, "The Skull", page 19.
<dl><dd>The Skull <a href = vnd.unilang:is.html>is</a> divided into two parts, the <b><a href = vnd.unilang:cranium.html>Cranium</a></b> and the Face. The <b><a href = vnd.unilang:cranium.html>Cranium</a></b> <a href = vnd.unilang:is.html>is</a> composed of <i>eight</i> <a href = vnd.unilang:bones.html>bones</a>; <span class="q-hellip-sp"> </span><span class="q-hellip-b">[</span>…<span class="q-hellip-b">]</span><span class="q-hellip-b"> </span></dd></dl></li>
<li><b>1908</b>, J. McFadyean, <i>The Comparative Anatomy of the Domesticated Animals</i>, Part I: Osteology and Arthrology, "Skeleton of Fowl", page 165.
<dl><dd>The <b><a href = vnd.unilang:cranium.html>cranium</a></b> of the fowl <a href = vnd.unilang:is.html>is</a> composed of the same elements as are present in the mammalian <b><a href = vnd.unilang:cranium.html>cranium</a></b>, save that there <a href = vnd.unilang:is.html>is</a> no interparietal.</dd></dl></li>
<li><b>2010</b>, <a href = vnd.unilang:elaine.html>Elaine</a> N. Marieb amp; <a href = vnd.unilang:katja.html>Katja</a> Hoehn, <i>Human Anatomy and Physiology</i>, 8th ed., Chapter 7 "The Skeleton", page 200
<dl><dd>The cranial <a href = vnd.unilang:bones.html>bones</a>, or <b><a href = vnd.unilang:cranium.html>cranium</a></b> (kra′ne-um), enclose and protect the fragile brain and furnish attachment sites for head and neck muscles.</dd></dl></li></ul></li>
<li><span class="ib-brac">(</span><span class="ib-content">anthropology<span class="ib-comma">,</span> informal</span><span class="ib-brac">)</span> The upper portion of the skull, including the neurocranium and facial <a href = vnd.unilang:bones.html>bones</a>, but not including the jawbone (mandible).
<ul><li><b>2014</b>, <a href = vnd.unilang:emma.html>Emma</a> L. Brown, Ronald A. Dixon, amp; Jason W. Birkett, "The Discolouration of Human Teeth from Archaeological Contexts: Elemental Analysis of a Black Tooth from a Roman <a href = vnd.unilang:cranium.html>Cranium</a> Recovered from the River Witham, Lincoln, UK", <i>Journal of Anthropology</i>, <a href = vnd.unilang:vol.html>vol</a>. 2014, Article ID 859153, 7 pages. <a rel="nofollow" class="external free" href="https://doi.org/10.1155/2014/859153">https://doi.org/10.1155/2014/859153</a>.
<dl><dd>In this study, the <b><a href = vnd.unilang:cranium.html>cranium</a></b> recovered from the River Witham in Lincoln exhibited a black metallic staining on the surfaces of the teeth.</dd></dl></li></ul></li>
<li><span class="ib-brac">(</span><span class="ib-content">informal</span><span class="ib-brac">)</span> The skull.</li></ol>
