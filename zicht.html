zicht (2486)


Etymology 1

From Middle Dutch sichte, from Proto-Germanic *sihtiz. This etymology is incomplete. You can help Wiktionary by elaborating on the origins of this term.

Noun

zicht n (plural zichten, diminutive zichtje n)

  1. sight, view, vision

Etymology 2

From Middle Dutch sichte (“short, light scythe”). Cognate to English scythe.

Noun

zicht f (plural zichten, diminutive zichtje n)

  1. sickle; a short scythe
