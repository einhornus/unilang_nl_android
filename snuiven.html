snuiven (12591)


Etymology

From Middle Dutch snuyven, from Old Dutch *snūvan, from Proto-Germanic *snūbaną (“to breathe, puff, blow, snort”), imitative of drawing air through the nose, related to Middle High German snūfen, German schnauben.

Verb

snuiven

  1. to sniff

Derived terms

  • snuiver
