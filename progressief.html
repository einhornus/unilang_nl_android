progressief (27472)


Etymology

Probably borrowed from French progressif, from Latin prōgressīvus.

Adjective

progressief (comparative progressiever, superlative progressiefst)

  1. progressive, pertaining to or favouring left-leaning, emancipatory or innovative politics, pertaining to or supporting progressivism [from 19th c.]
  2. progressive, pertaining to progress [from early 19th c.]
  3. progressive, which progresses (e.g. becomes increasingly severe)
  4. progressive, placing proportionally higher burdens in relation to a quantity (e.g. income, wealth), often in relations to taxes or rates
  5. progressive, pertaining to subsequent elements [from 19th c.]

Inflection

Related terms

  • progressie
  • progressivisme
  • progressiviteit

Bijvoeglijk naamwoord

progressief
