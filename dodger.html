dodger (21101)


Etymology

dodge + | -er.

Noun

dodger (plural dodgers)

Dodger on a small sailing boat
  1. Someone who dodges.
  2. (nautical) A frame-supported canvas over the companionway (entrance) of a sailboat providing the on-deck crew partial cover from the splashes of the seas that break against the hull of the boat.
  3. (Australia, slang) An advertising leaflet; a flyer.
  4. (Australia, US, slang, dated) bread.

Synonyms

  • (companionway cover): sprayhood
