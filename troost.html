troost (3918)


Etymology

From Middle Dutch troost, from Old Dutch trōst, from Proto-Germanic *traustą.

Noun

troost m (uncountable)

  1. A comfort, consolation

Related terms

  • troosteloos (adjective)

Verb

troost

  1. first-, second- and third-person singular present indicative of troosten
  2. imperative of troosten
