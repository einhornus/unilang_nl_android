grollen (69765)


Etymology

From Middle High German grollen (“to debase; lower; humiliate”), from Old High German *grollen, from Proto-Germanic *gruljaną (“to yell; shout at”), from Proto-Indo-European *gʰer- (“to grumble”). Related to German gellen (“to shout”), compare Dutch grillen (“to shiver, shake”), English grill (“to provoke”) and growl.

Verb

grollen (third-person singular simple present grollt, past tense grollte, past participle gegrollt, auxiliary haben)

  1. (transitive) to grumble, to thunder
  2. (transitive) to be angry
