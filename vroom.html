vroom (20643)


Etymology

From Middle Dutch vrōme (“firm, upright”), an adjective derived from the noun vrōme (“benefit, use”), from Old Dutch *fruma, from Proto-Germanic *frumô.

Adjective

vroom (comparative vromer, superlative vroomst)

  1. pious, devout
    Synonym: godvruchtig

Inflection
