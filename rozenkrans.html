rozenkrans (16480)


Etymology

From Middle Dutch rosencrans. Equivalent to roos (“rose”) + | krans (“wreath”).

Noun

rozenkrans m (plural rozenkransen, diminutive rozenkransje n)

  1. (literally) A wreath of roses
  2. The rosary, a Catholic prayer sequence, consisting of repetitions of Our Lady and Our Father
  3. A rosary, Catholic or, less commonly, Anglican prayer beads used to keep track of the above
  4. An object resembling the wreath:
    1. a deer's antlers' knobby edges
    2. the plant Gnaphalium dioicum, now Antennaria dioica
  5. A symbolical meaning of the wreath:
    1. youth
    2. virginity

Synonyms

  • (prayer sequence) rozenhoedje n
  • (prayer beads) paternoster m
